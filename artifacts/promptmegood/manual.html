🚀 1. Getting started

No signup. No download. The whole workstation runs in your browser, and your work stays on your device until you choose to send something to an AI provider.

Open the app for the first time

  1. Visit promptmegood.com and click Open The App → in the top-right corner of the landing page.
  2. The workstation opens at /app. The first time you visit, you'll see a short tour and a Use Demo Values button — accept it to walk through every part of the builder, or close it and start typing.
  3. Pick your theme using the moon/sun toggle in the top bar. We mirror your OS preference by default.

Open the app as a returning user

  1. Visit promptmegood.com again. If you've used the app before, you'll be redirected straight to /app.
  2. At the top of the workstation you'll see a Welcome Back panel with three primary actions: Continue Last Prompt, Start A New Prompt, and Open Workspace.
  3. Six secondary links underneath jump straight into Full Builder, Image Prompt, Prompt Vault, Prompt History, and Templates.
Want to bypass the redirect? Visit /?stay=1 to view the marketing landing page even if you're a returning user.

🪟 2. The workstation layout

PromptMeGood arranges everything into a calm three-area chassis. Each area has one job.

Top bar

App brand on the left, navigation links in the center (Builder · Vault · Templates · Pricing · Guide), and your Theme, Accent, and Open Expert Command Center controls on the right. The top bar is always visible.

Left rail — Vault & Templates

Your saved prompts, recent history, tags, and the templates picker all live here. On phones (under 900 px wide) the rail collapses into a bottom dock with two tabs: Vault and Templates.

Center — Builder & Conversation

The main work surface. Top of column: the goal box, mode pills, settings, and the Generate Prompt button. Below: Step 1 — Prompt Tuning, then (after generation) Step 2 — Your Fixed Prompt, and the Run With AI response panel.

Right — Visual Asset Engine

Step 3 — Photography Suite and Step 4 — Image Generator live here when you switch to Create-an-Image mode. On smaller screens this stacks below the builder.

Status bar

Bottom edge of the workstation. Shows the current mode, your accent color, and a small indicator that confirms when a prompt is auto-saved to the vault.

✍️ 3. Building a text prompt

Text mode is the default. Switch to image mode any time using the pills at the top of the builder — your text and settings are kept across modes.

Step 1: Type your goal

  1. Click into the big text area labeled What do you want AI to help you with?
  2. Type one honest sentence. "Help me write a cold email to a SaaS founder who ignored my last note."
  3. Watch the strength pill above the box: it cycles through Too Short → Getting There → Good → Strong ✓ as you add detail. You can generate at any strength.

Step 2: (Optional) Help Me Start

  1. Tap the green Help Me Start button at the top of the builder.
  2. Answer four short questions: What you need help with · Who it's for · The outcome that matters most · Any rules or limits.
  3. Use the Back link if you need to revisit any answer.
  4. Tap Done. The Goal, Extra Details, and Rules Or Limits fields fill in automatically.
  5. Review, tweak anything, then continue to Generate.

Step 3: Adjust the form fields

All of these are optional. Skipping them is fine — defaults are reasonable.

Category

Tells the builder which kind of problem you're solving. Pick the closest fit; choose Other if nothing fits.

  • Business — strategy, sales, operations
  • Money — income ideas, profit, fast action
  • Content — hooks, scripts, storytelling
  • Career — resumes, interviews, growth
  • Personal — decisions, relationships, life
  • Productivity — focus, habits, systems
  • Learning — explanations, practice, skills
  • Faith — Scripture-focused guidance
  • Other — anything that doesn't fit

Experience Level

Beginner simplifies. Intermediate skips basics. Advanced goes deep on strategy.

Tone

Professional, Bold & Direct, Casual, or Expert. Match it to where you'll use the answer — Bold reads great in marketing copy, Professional fits internal docs.

Output Format

Step-by-step for action, List for fast scanning, Detailed Breakdown for deeper learning, Plain Paragraph for emails and copy.

Output Language

Pick the language for the AI's reply. The interface stays in English. Currently available: English, Spanish, Portuguese, Russian, French, German.

Personality Mode

Adds a specific voice to the AI's reply. Try a few to see what fits — they pair well with Tone.

  • No Personality Added — neutral baseline
  • Direct & Straightforward — no fluff
  • Friendly & Conversational — relaxed
  • Bold & Persuasive — convincing
  • Professional & Structured — polished
  • Creative & Expressive — imaginative
  • Faith-Based & Convicting — spiritual clarity
  • Street-Smart & Practical — real-world
  • Luxury Brand Voice — premium
  • Viral Social Media Voice — punchy

Extra Details

Optional. Add budget, timeline, platform, audience, or constraints. Every extra detail sharpens the answer.

Max Response Length

Controls how long the AI's reply is. Pick a preset (100, 200, 300, or 500 words), choose Custom to type any number, or leave it on No Limit for full-length answers.

Rules Or Limits

Tell the AI what not to do. Examples: "don't sound robotic", "avoid jargon", "don't recommend paid ads". These get added to the prompt as explicit limits.

Optional boosts

Toggle these on to shape the response. They stack — combine for stronger results.

  • Money Mode — biases toward profit, ROI, and conversion-focused angles
  • Human Tone — strips out robotic AI patterns
  • Clarity Boost — favors short sentences and concrete language
  • Auto Optimize — runs an extra pass to refine the prompt before showing it

Step 4: Upload a file (optional)

  1. Open the Upload A File Or Image For Smarter Results control inside the builder.
  2. Tap Choose File and pick a PDF, JPG, or PNG (up to 10 MB).
  3. Type a goal — for example, "Summarise this contract in plain English."
  4. Click Analyze My File. The AI's response appears underneath.
Privacy: uploaded files plus your goal are sent to the AI provider for the request. We do not store the file on our servers after the request completes. See the Privacy Policy.

Step 5: Generate the prompt

  1. Click the big primary Generate Prompt button at the bottom of the builder.
  2. Your structured prompt appears in the output box on the right (or below on mobile) within a couple of seconds.
  3. If you want to see what a finished prompt looks like before typing anything yourself, hit Use Demo Values — it loads a sample goal and replays the guided tour.

🔧 4. Refining and running

Once a prompt has been generated, the Step 2 — Your Fixed Prompt panel appears with refinement controls and the Run With AI button.

Tone shifters & Undo

The featured row underneath the prompt shows the most-used refiners. Tap one and the prompt is rewritten in place.

  • More Detailed — adds context, audience, and constraints
  • More Bold & Direct — strips hedging language
  • Beginner Friendly — simplifies the framing
  • Undo Last Change — steps back any refinement

Less-used refiners (More Casual, More Concise, Add Examples, etc.) live in a secondary pill row to keep things calm.

Run With AI

  1. With a prompt visible, tap the ▶ Run With AI button.
  2. The AI response streams into the panel labeled AI Response directly underneath your prompt.
  3. When it finishes, use Copy Response to take it elsewhere or Run Again to retry.
Privacy: only your optimized prompt text is sent to the AI provider. The response is shown only on your device.

Check Prompt Quality

  1. Look at the Prompt Strength score above the prompt — it grades structure, specificity, and constraints out of 100.
  2. Tap Check Prompt Quality to see two or three concrete suggestions for tightening the prompt.
  3. Apply any suggestion, then re-run.

Use the prompt elsewhere

  1. Tap Copy Prompt.
  2. The Send-to row underneath highlights the best-fit destination — ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity — and offers Open in ChatGPT, Open in Claude, etc.
  3. Or tap the 🖨 Print icon for an offline copy.

Save to Vault

  1. Tap Save To Vault. A small toast confirms the save.
  2. The prompt now appears in the left rail under Vault, with the goal as its title.
  3. Add tags, pin, rename, or delete from the rail at any time.

🎨 5. Image generation

Switch to image mode to compose a photographic brief and render it with DALL·E 3 — no copy-paste between tools.

Switch into image mode

  1. At the top of the builder, tap the 🎨 Create An Image pill.
  2. The goal label becomes Your Image, the Photography Suite appears, and the Generate button becomes Generate Image.
  3. Type what you want to see in the goal box — subject, scene, mood. One sentence is enough.

Step 3 — Photography Suite

Five collapsible groups of pills sit to the right of the builder. Tap pills to compose; only the Style group is open by default to save scroll space.

Style

Photographic, Cinematic, Editorial, Documentary, Studio Portrait, Product Hero, Street, Fine Art, and more. Pick one as the foundation.

Camera & Lens

Body type (35mm, medium format, mirrorless), lens length (24mm wide, 85mm portrait, 200mm telephoto), and Pro knobs for aperture, shutter speed, ISO, and film stock.

Lighting & Mood

Golden hour, blue hour, soft window light, hard side light, neon, candlelit, overcast, etc.

Composition

Rule of thirds, centered, leading lines, low angle, top-down, close crop, wide environmental, etc.

Color Palette

Warm earth tones, cool teal & orange, monochrome, pastel, jewel tones, high contrast B&W, and more.

Surprise Me & Clear Picks

  1. Surprise Me — rolls a random pick across all groups. Great for breaking out of a creative rut.
  2. Clear Picks — wipes every selection so you can start over.

Step 4 — Generate the image

  1. Tap Send To Image Generator in the Photography Suite. We synthesize the photo brief and pass it to DALL·E 3 in one shot.
  2. You'll see a Generating Image — This Can Take A Few Moments spinner. Both the generate and regenerate buttons disable while the request is in flight.
  3. When the image arrives, the panel shows Your Image Is Ready above it.

Work with the image

  • Download — saves the PNG to your device.
  • Regenerate — runs the same brief again with a fresh seed.
  • Use As Reference — copies a description of the image to your clipboard so you can paste it back into the Prompt Builder for follow-up work.

🗂 6. The Prompt Vault

Every prompt you save lives in the left rail. The vault is local-first — it lives in your browser and never leaves unless you export it.

Save a prompt

  1. Generate a prompt.
  2. Tap Save To Vault. A small toast confirms the save.
  3. The prompt appears in the left rail with the goal as its title.

Find an old prompt

  1. Open the rail (it's always open on desktop; tap the Vault tab on the bottom dock on phones).
  2. Type into the search box at the top of the rail. Search runs across goal text, tags, and any extra details.
  3. Or use ⌘+K to open the global command palette and search vault items from anywhere.

Tag, pin, rename, or delete

  1. Click any vault item to open it.
  2. Use the small actions row in the rail item to Pin, Rename, Add Tag, or Delete.
  3. Pinned items always sort to the top.

Compare two prompts

  1. Open one prompt from the rail.
  2. Use the rail item's Compare action on a second prompt — both open side-by-side in the center panel.
  3. Use this to see exactly what a refinement changed.

Export your vault

  1. Open the rail's ⋯ menu and choose Export Vault.
  2. You'll get a JSON file containing every prompt, tag, and timestamp.
  3. Use Import to load a previously-exported file on a new browser or device.

📋 7. Templates

28+ starter templates cover the most common prompt jobs — cold emails, content briefs, study guides, product specs, and more.

Open the templates picker

  1. Click the Choose A Starting Template… picker at the top of the builder.
  2. The templates panel opens in the left rail. On phones the bottom dock flips to the Vault tab so the picker is visible.
  3. Search by name or scroll the list — categories are color-coded.

Apply a template

  1. Click any template card. The picker label updates to show your selection.
  2. The Goal, Extra Details, Tone, Format, and other fields fill in automatically.
  3. Edit anything you want, then tap Generate Prompt.

Save your own template

  1. Build any prompt the usual way.
  2. Open the Expert Command Center and switch to the Save tab.
  3. Give the template a name and short description, then save. It now appears in the picker under My Templates.

⚙️ 8. Expert Command Center

A right-side drawer with five tabs — Diagnose, Engineer, Tune, Variations, and Save. It's free for everyone during the open beta and a paid feature after.

Open the Command Center

  1. Click the ⚙ Expert Mode toggle in the top bar of the workstation.
  2. The first three times you open it, you'll see a quick warning explaining that Expert Mode unlocks deeper controls. Tap Open Anyway to confirm.
  3. The drawer slides in from the right. Switch tabs along the top.

Diagnose

Runs a structured analysis of your current prompt: clarity, specificity, persona, format, output length, and likely failure modes. Shows a numeric score and a short list of fixes.

Engineer

Step-by-step rewriting controls. Add a system message, tweak the persona, force JSON output, define few-shot examples, or chain multiple steps.

Tune

Adjust how creative or how strict the AI's answer should be, and cap the response length. Useful when you want a more imaginative reply or a tightly-bounded one.

Variations

Generates three or four parallel versions of the same prompt — short, long, formal, casual — so you can pick the strongest framing.

Save

Save the current prompt as a template, a workflow, or a brand voice profile (Pro). Workflows let you replay multi-step prompt chains with a single click.

Beta status. While the open beta is live, Expert Command Center is free for all users. After the beta ends, it becomes a paid feature included with Founding Member and Pro plans.

🎛 9. Settings & personalization

Most preferences are one click away in the top bar. Everything is stored locally; nothing syncs to a server.

Theme

  1. Click the moon/sun toggle in the top bar.
  2. Choose Light or Dark. Defaults to your OS preference.

Accent color

  1. Open the accent picker in the top bar (small color dots).
  2. Choose Green (default), Blue, Purple, Gold, or Slate.
  3. The accent re-skins every primary control — picker pulses, buttons, focus rings — and is saved across pages and reloads.

Voice input

  1. Click the microphone icon next to the goal box.
  2. Grant microphone permission to your browser if prompted.
  3. Choose your input language from the small dropdown next to the mic.
  4. Speak naturally. The transcript fills the goal box live.

Brand voice profiles (Pro)

  1. Open the Expert Command Center and switch to the Save tab.
  2. Choose Save As Brand Voice.
  3. Give the voice a name and a short description (tone, words to avoid, signature phrases).
  4. Apply it later from the same tab before generating a prompt.

Mobile dock

Below 900 px wide, the left rail collapses into a bottom dock with two tabs — Vault and Templates. Tap either to bring the rail content into view.

🔒 12. Privacy & data

Local-first by default. Your prompts, vault, settings, and theme live in your browser. Only the data you actively send to an AI provider ever leaves your device.

What stays on your device

  • The Vault (every saved prompt, tag, and timestamp)
  • Templates you've created
  • Theme, accent, language, and other settings
  • Brand voice profiles
  • Workflow definitions

What gets sent to AI providers

  • Generate Prompt — your goal text plus any settings you chose
  • Run With AI — the optimized prompt text
  • Analyze My File — the uploaded file plus your goal text
  • Generate Image — the synthesized photo brief
  • Expert Command Center tabs — the prompt content for the chosen analysis

Files uploaded for analysis are not stored on our servers after the request completes. AI provider responses are returned only to your device. See the Privacy Policy for the full details, including GDPR / UK GDPR / CCPA rights, data retention, and AI-image copyright clarification.

🛠 13. Troubleshooting

The page looks stale or styles look broken

  1. Hard-refresh: ⌘+Shift+R (Mac) or Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows / Linux).
  2. If that doesn't help, open your browser settings and clear the cache for promptmegood.com.
  3. Reload the page. Your vault and settings are unaffected — they live in localStorage, not the cache.

I'm a returning user but want to see the marketing page

Visit /?stay=1. The redirect to /app is bypassed for that load.

The Expert Command Center won't open

  1. Confirm you're logged in to a paid plan if the open beta has ended (it's free during beta).
  2. Check that no other dialog is open — close it with Esc first.
  3. If the warning dialog appears for a moment and then disappears, hard-refresh once and try again.

Run With AI returns nothing or an error

  1. Check that you haven't hit the daily fair-use limit. Free-tier limits reset every 24 hours.
  2. Try shortening the prompt — extremely long prompts can time out.
  3. If the error persists, copy the prompt and paste it into ChatGPT or Claude directly to confirm whether the prompt itself is the issue.

My vault is empty after switching browsers

The vault is local-first and lives in your browser's localStorage. To move your vault between browsers or devices, use Export Vault on the original browser and Import on the new one. We're not able to recover vaults — please export periodically as a backup.

My image won't generate

  1. Confirm you're in image mode (the goal label should read Your Image).
  2. Make sure you've described the image in the goal box. Empty briefs are rejected.
  3. Try simplifying the brief — extremely complex Photography Suite combinations can occasionally fail. Use Clear Picks and try again.

Still stuck?

Email support@promptmegood.com with a screenshot and a brief description. We read every message.
