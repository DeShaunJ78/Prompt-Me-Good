The Right Widget Strategy for Your Situation
The widget should be free, limited, and branded. Here is exactly what that means.

Free means no API key required for the host site. Anyone can paste the script tag and it works. This is how you get distribution — friction kills adoption. PromptBase requires approval. PromptPerfect requires a developer. You just work.

Limited means the widget only runs the generate endpoint, not Run With AI. Users get the engineered prompt. To run it through AI and see the response, they click a “Run in PromptMeGood →” button that opens your app. That is your conversion funnel. The widget is the top of it.

Branded means the widget has a small “Powered by PromptMeGood” badge. Every embed is a backlink and a brand impression. This is how PromptMeGood gets discovered by the PromptPerfect migration wave — their users land on sites that already have your widget embedded.


The Daily Cap That Protects You
Add a per-domain daily cap of 500 widget calls stored server-side by the referring domain. No API key needed from the host — you track it by `Origin` header. At $0.004 each, 500 calls = $2.00/day per domain. You can absorb that. If a domain goes viral and hits the cap, widget calls return a graceful “Try again tomorrow or sign up free” message with a link to PMG.

A global widget cap of $10/day across all domains gives you 2,500 widget calls per day before the circuit breaker fires. That is plenty for launch. Raise it as revenue grows.
