
My honest take on the generation takeover idea:

The instinct is right. The current “Generating your prompt… please wait” state is good but it’s a missed opportunity. A full-screen takeover during generation would make the product feel more premium and intentional.

What I’d actually build, and why it’s different from what was proposed:

The other AI suggested 3 variants (A/B/C) and asked you to pick. That’s a lot of decisions before anything is proven. I’d suggest one well-executed version first, then iterate based on how it feels.

The version I’d build: a clean full-screen takeover that echoes the user’s goal back to them (“Tuning your prompt for: catch fish for the first time…”) with 2–3 rotating status lines that feel like the AI is actually thinking — “Analyzing your goal”, “Selecting tone and format”, “Engineering your prompt” — and a subtle animated progress indicator. Not a spinner, not a progress bar, just a gentle pulsing glow on the status text. Fades in over 200ms, fades out when done.

What I’d skip from the proposal:

The “cinematic” option with layered motion and bold typography. For a utility tool like PromptMeGood, cinematic feels mismatched — users are here to get work done, not watch a show. Subtle-to-premium is the right register.

The split-screen “goal on left / tuning steps on right” idea is visually interesting but adds complexity for no functional gain on mobile.

The cancel/escape hatch the other AI flagged is genuinely important and I’d make it a first-class part of the build, not an afterthought.


Before I build anything though — I want to make sure we’re not repeating the pattern from the overlay work. That started as a good idea too, and it broke things because the implementation touched too many moving parts at once.

This takeover is actually simpler than the overlay was — it’s a CSS overlay that appears during generation and disappears when done. No DOM node moving, no restoring, no cloning. Much lower risk.

My proposal: I read the generation flow code first, understand exactly when the “generating” state starts and ends, then build the takeover in one clean pass. No mockup phase — just build it right the first time and you can see it live.
