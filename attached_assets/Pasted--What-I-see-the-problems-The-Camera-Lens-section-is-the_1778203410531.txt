
What I see — the problems:
The Camera & Lens section is the worst offender. It currently dumps 28 pills in a single undifferentiated blob: lens focal lengths, camera bodies, aperture values, shutter speeds, ISO values, and film stocks are all mixed together with no visual separation. A user staring at 85mm Portrait, DSLR, f/1.4 Bokeh, 1/60 Motion Blur, ISO 100 Clean, and Kodak Portra 400 side by side has no idea what they are choosing or why.
The same problem exists in Style — Quick Snap presets (Cinematic, Editorial) are mixed in with the full style pill grid, so the hierarchy is unclear.
The overall structure also has a cognitive load problem: every section is expanded by default, showing every pill at once. On mobile this is an endless scroll of 80+ pills with no clear starting point.
My recommendation — three changes:
1. Sub-group the Camera & Lens section into labeled rows.
Instead of one flat blob, split it into clearly labeled sub-rows:
Sub-group
Pills
Focal Length
14mm Ultra-Wide, 24mm Wide, 35mm Wide, 50mm Standard, 85mm Portrait, 200mm Telephoto
Camera Body
DSLR, Mirrorless, Film Grain, Drone Aerial, GoPro Action, iPhone Snap
Aperture
f/1.4 Bokeh, f/2.8 Soft, f/8 Sharp, f/16 Deep DOF
Shutter Speed
1/60 Motion Blur, 1/250 Sharp, 1/1000 Frozen Action
ISO
ISO 100 Clean, ISO 400 Daylight, ISO 1600 Low-Light
Film Stock
Kodak Portra 400, CineStill 800T, Tri-X B&W
Each sub-group gets a small grey label above it — just a text label, not another accordion. This turns 28 random pills into a scannable, logical grid.
2. Move the Quick Snap presets above the pill grid in every section.
The preset buttons (Cinematic, Editorial, Golden Hour etc.) are the fastest path for most users. They should sit in a clearly separated "Quick Pick" row at the top of each section, visually distinct from the individual pills below. Right now they blend in and get ignored.
3. Collapse all sections to just their header by default on mobile.
Show only the section header (e.g., "📷 Camera & Lens ▾") until the user taps to expand. This reduces the initial view to 5 clean section headers — the user picks the one they care about, expands it, makes their selection, and moves on. No endless scroll.
